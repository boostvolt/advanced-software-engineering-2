
# Facilitator Notes

This activity is designed to run quickly. The tone should be analytical rather than theatrical. The class should feel that they are being asked to make an engineering decision, not to guess the “correct” opinion about AI.

## Suggested Opening Script

“In our project, one of the main things we found is that AI-assisted software engineering is not just about code generation. It is about whether generated output can be trusted in context. In a complex system, a suggestion can look polished and still be incomplete, unsafe, or misleading. We want to test that idea with a very short exercise.”

## Timing

Spend about two minutes introducing the exercise and distributing the handout. Give groups four minutes to discuss their assigned scenario. Use the next three minutes to collect one response from each group. Use the final minute to connect their answers back to the project.

## Debrief Message

The key lesson is that the more trustworthy review is usually the one that slows the team down just enough to ask the right validation question. This does not mean AI assistance is bad. It means that AI changes the workflow. Drafting becomes easier, but review becomes more important. That is why our project argues that AI-assisted SWE is best understood as a productivity amplifier under constraints rather than a substitute for engineering judgment.

## Link Back to the Project

If there is time, connect the discussion explicitly to three findings from the project. First, AI can improve speed and reduce friction on bounded tasks. Second, polished output can still be wrong or incomplete. Third, the real challenge in large systems is not only generating solutions, but validating them under architectural, security, and organizational constraints.
