# ASE2 Project Task Specification

## Project description

**Main goal:** Dive into the main challenges of large complex software systems in practice.

In this project, the students work in teams to explore and explain a specific challenge in large complex software systems from industry.

The main tasks for this project and their corresponding percentage for grading is presented below.

## Tasks and grading percentages

### Task 1: Select a topic and motivation

**1.1.** Select a topic and find literature related to it.

You can suggest a topic and literature. Contact the lecturers for approval. We support these areas/topics:

- Software modeling
- AI-assisted software engineering
- Sustainable software engineering
- Real-time/embedded software engineering

**1.2.** Study your topic and identify main motivations. For this:

- List the main challenges and topics that are discussed in the chapter.
- Do research about your chapter and report at least 2 use cases from industries and/or domains who struggle and/or have proposed solutions for the challenges, activities, and difficulties presented in your chapter.

**1.3.** Submit a draft summarising the results from tasks 1.1 and 1.2 via Moodle before the deadline.

### Task 2: Implement a Case Study

Investigate existing solutions (i.e., tools, frameworks, methods, etc) to address the challenges and difficulties presented in the chapter of your choice. Select one solution and apply it to a case study of your choice* to demonstrate trade-offs and existing gaps.

- List existing solutions from your investigation
- Make an implementation to show how the selected solution is applied to your case study.
- Create a demonstration that you can later show in front of the class
- Reflect on the following questions:
  - Why did you select the solution for your case study? Which solutions were discarded and why?
  - How was the experience of implementing the solution to your case study? What are the main difficulties you have confronted?
  - Which are the major trade-offs you observed?
  - Which existing gaps are prevalent? Can they be solved? Which solutions do you envision?

*Possible case studies:*

- Follow-up project from ASE1
- PM1, PM2, PM3, PM4 projects
- Personal project under FOSS license
- A project from an open repository.

\* Company projects are not allowed. The projects should be open source, to be accessed by students and lecturers of ASE2.

### Task 3: Create a short report (Mandatory)

Compile the results and information from tasks 1 and 2 to create a short report of 2-5 pages. Consider the following structure for your report:

- Context and motivation: situate and motivate your topic and case study.
- Question/problem: formulate the specific gaps and trade-offs.
- Principal ideas/results: summarize the ideas and results. State the solutions you have used and the reasoning behind your decisions. State limitations you have found.

Submit your short report before the deadline via Moodle (see weekly plan in Moodle).

### Task 4: Create an activity for the class

Create an interactive activity for the class to share the topics and main concepts from your selected topic. Some possible activities can be a quiz, panel, game, etc.

The activity should last approximately 10 minutes.

### Task 5: Prepare a presentation

Make a Power Point presentation to show the results of the tasks 1, 2, and 4 at the presentations day (see weekly plan in Moodle).

**Content (25 minutes):**

- Input (10 minutes)
  - A catchy title and the names of the students
  - Main results of task 1
  - Main results of task 2
  - Discuss 5 main lessons learnt
- Lead an activity for the class (task 4) (10 minutes)
- Discussion by another team (5 minutes)

**What is expected during the presentation?**

For this presentation, you must be clear about what the intended audience of your presentation is. Remember, this is a presentation that describes your experience in studying and implementing solutions for an ASE challenge. Your intended audience are academic people with industry experience (your fellow students and teachers). During the presentation, the groups of students elaborate on their work with the visual aid of digital slides. During the session, students present to one another their work. If the students have special ideas for their presentation or would like to make use of extra support (flipcharts, blackboard, posters, post-it, etc.), feel free to bring it over and/or ask your teachers for support.

### Task 6: Prepare the discussion slide for another team's report

Each short report is assigned to a discussant team. Discussant teams give a critical review of the assigned short report directly after its presentation by presenting one slide that provides answers to a predefined set of questions (see below). The assignments of discussants will be made available to the teams one week before the presentations day (see weekly plan in Moodle).

- What do we now know we did not know before?
- Are the reported conclusions generalizable?
- In which way can you relate to the lessons learned?
- Pick one of the following intentionally controversial questions to answer in your discussion:
  - What is the most questionable issue of the report?
  - Why is the experience not very representative?

The discussion should last at most 5 minutes.

## Submission

Submit your short report, presentation, discussion slide and code repository (link) via Moodle before the deadline.

## Grading criteria

The following tasks will be evaluated through the short report, presentation, discussion slide and code repository.
Active participation and clear contribution by each team member is a requirement to get a grade for the project.

**Tasks 1 — 20%**
- Results presented in an understandable and comprehensible way
- Results critically discussed, suggestions for improvement

**Task 2 — 30%**
- Presents a clear reasoning behind the selection of solutions
- Own experiences are presented, no summaries of generic data from ChatGPT
- Trade-offs, limitations, and gaps are presented. There is a reflection regarding the state of the practice, as well as the experience from the students.

**Task 3 — Mandatory**
- This task is mandatory and must be submitted before the deadline in order to receive a grade for the project.
- The short report is complete regarding content.
- The content is focused to the essentials and properly summarize the results from tasks 1 and 2.
- The report includes interesting reflections and reflect the results in a compact and comprehensible manner

**Task 4 — 20%**
- The activity is related to the topic selected
- The activity is original, creative and engaging
- The activity is inclusive and integrates all students and teachers

**Task 5 — 15%**
- Professional appearance, clarity, language
- Clear structure, restriction to essentials
- Professional handling of questions

**Task 6 — 15%**
- Present a concrete summary of the short report
- Bring constructive feedback
